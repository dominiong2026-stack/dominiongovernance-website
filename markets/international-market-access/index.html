International Market Access Readiness

AI governance is increasingly relevant to market access. Organisations that cannot explain AI use, data controls, human oversight and evidence may face avoidable friction with sophisticated clients and counterparties.

Client and procurement questions

Prepare answers on AI tools, data handling, confidentiality, employee use, vendor exposure and approval discipline.

Investor and insurer scrutiny

Show that AI risk is visible, owned, controlled and reported rather than treated as informal experimentation.

Board and executive assurance

Create board-ready summaries, control maps, evidence packs and action trackers that support informed oversight.

Responsible positioning

Build confidence without overclaiming certification, legal compliance, audit assurance or technical guarantees.

Typical readiness outputs

  • AI governance readiness snapshot.
  • External-question response pack.
  • Data exposure and confidentiality themes.
  • Risk-control-evidence map.
  • 90-day improvement roadmap.
